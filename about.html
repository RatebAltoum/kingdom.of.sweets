The importance of sweets in Arab culture

Sweets have an important role in Arab culture, as they are considered part of the traditions and celebrations in Arab society. The names of Arabic sweets vary from one place to another. They are also considered a sign of generosity and hospitality that shows concern for house guests and strengthens social ties between individuals. Sweets are also used to celebrate religious occasions. Social events such as Eid al-Fitr and the holy month of Ramadan.

In addition, it is part of the Arab hospitality tradition, as offering sweets to guests is a token of respect and appreciation, and it is also part of the gifts that people give to each other on various occasions.


The most famous types of Arabic sweets

Arabic sweets are famous for their variety and wonderful taste. These sweets rely on the use of nuts such as almonds, pistachios, and walnuts, as well as honey to add a special flavor. Among the most famous types of Arabic sweets are the following:

Kunafa:

1-Kunafa dessert is considered famous in the Levant and Egypt, and is often served on occasions and celebrations. There are several types of Kunafa, such as Nabulsi, Mabrouma, Kunafa with cream, and Kunafa with cheese.

2-Kunafa with cheese is prepared using ready-made kunafa dough and grated cheese.

3-Kunafa dessert is usually served with Arabic coffee, and is considered a delicious snack consumed at social occasions and celebrations.

4-They are sweetened with syrup or powdered sugar, and roasted in the oven until they become golden and crispy.

Qatayef:

1-A delicious dessert that is famous in many Arab countries and the Middle East, and its origins go back to Syria.

2-It is considered one of the traditional sweets that is specially prepared during the holy month of Ramadan.

3-Qatayef consists of thin dough stuffed with a filling of walnuts, pistachios, or cream, then fried until golden, and drizzled with syrup.

4-Eating Qatayef dessert after breakfast with family and friends is a beautiful tradition that brings people together and strengthens social ties.

Baklava:

1-Baklava dessert is one of the most famous types of oriental desserts, distinguished by its thin layers and rich filling.

2-There are many types of baklava, including pistachio baklava, chocolate baklava, and milk baklava.

3-It reflects a rich heritage of oriental sweets that are distinguished by their exquisite taste and unique flavour.

4-Baklava consists of sheets of thin dough that are stuffed with nuts such as walnuts or almonds and spread with butter and syrup.

Balah Al-Sham:

1-This dessert is considered one of the traditional sweets in some Eastern and Arab cultures, and is distinguished by its sweet and delicious taste.

2-It was given the name “Balah al-Sham” due to its similarity in shape to dates. The dough is formed and dipped in sugar before eating it. This dessert has spread in Egypt, the Levant, Iraq, and Turkey.

3-Preparing balah is a process that many families carry out in their homes on a daily basis or on special occasions.
